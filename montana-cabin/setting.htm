    The house is located on 60 secluded acres along a river's edge. The property contains an extensive wooded area and a 40 acre alfalfa pasture for grazing cows. The natural setting is ideal for raising horses. An Amish colony borders the property on one side and a working ranch is on the other. A conservation easement insures protection and plenty of privacy for this natural treasure, further enhanced by being in a reparian zone.

Included on the property is a charming 1900's Native American log cabin. Its natural beauty is so striking that it was featured on the cover of the local telephone book.

Out of the snow capped mountains, a majestic waterfall rushes into the property's Mission River, and fish, waterfowl, deer and bear are in abundance.
