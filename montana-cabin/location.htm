    The house is located in the magnificent Mission Valley 40 miles north of Missoula and 25 miles south of Flathead Lake, near the small historic town of Saint Ignatius.

Located near by are:

  • Cross country skiing out back door
  • Settled Amish community with horse drawn transport throughout area
  • Great hikes to Mission Lake and waterfall out back door, 2 miles
  • Airplane landing strip, 3 miles
  • 2 18 hole golf courses, 19 to 25 miles
  • Flathead Lake, 23 miles
  • 4 ski runs within 50 to 80 miles
  
     
  St. Ignatius is beautifully situated in the Mission Valley bounded by the Mission mountains to the east and the hills of the National Bison Range to the west. The area was developed in the mid 1850’s combining three different tribes and westward bound homesteaders, allocating land to each of them. Consequently, the approximately 1/3 tribal, 2/3 non tribal area provides an exceptional environment in which to live. The reservation extends midway into the 28 mile Flathead Lake.

The Jesuit Mission, built in 1891, is located in the heart of the community, is listed on the National Historic Registry. In it are 58 stunning original murals by Brother Jose Carignano.

    
     
  On The National Bison Range, 8 miles S roam over 500 Bison on 20,000 acres of grassland. Year around, one is likely to see elk, bighorn sheep, antelope and deer as well as the herd of buffalo.

The Pablo National Wildlife Refuge, N of the range, is an exceptional wetland complex and prime for bird watching.

The Salish, Pend d’ Oreille, and Kootenai tribes of the Flathead Reservation have inhabited western Montana and the surrounding region for thousands of years. They are a culturally rich community of gentle people. Throughout the area, the tribe coordinates a Cultural Center, a 4 year college, annual pow wows, art galleries and a convention center in Polson on Flathead Lake. They successfully manage the tribal hunting and fishing habitat, ensuring continuous respect for the land.

An Amish Community of around 200 live in and around the St. Ignatius area. They operate a weekly farmstyle restaurant and their charming horse drawn buggies can be seen daily. They are stewards of the land and share in the environmental protection of the area.

A plane landing strip for a small twin is situated 3 miles N, and the Missoula International Airport is 40 miles S. An 18 hole golf course is 19 miles N.

Guided pack trips into the Missions and the Bob Marshall Wilderness, river floats, fishing expeditions on the Flathead River and rodeos, make this area a spectacular place to live.
